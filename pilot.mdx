This document outlines the response flags for the latest Alias version.

## Automated Test: Gibberish
- **Question:** "What is your favorite book and why?"
- **Example Response:** "asdfghjkl"
- **Interpretation:** Such responses lack intelligible content, highlighting a failure in respondent engagement or comprehension.

## Automated Test: Off-topic
- **Question:** "How do you approach problem-solving?"
- **Example Response:** "I love going to the beach on sunny days."
- **Interpretation:** Off-topic responses do not correlate with the query's intent, suggesting a misalignment in understanding or focus.

## Automated Test: Low-effort
- **Question:** "Describe a challenging project you worked on."
- **Example Response:** "It was hard, but I did it."
- **Interpretation:** Responses that lack elaboration provide limited insights, underscoring the importance of engagement metrics in your API toolkit.

## Automated Test: GPT
- **Question:** "What strategies do you use to manage stress?"
- **Example Response:** "Regular exercise, healthy eating, and sufficient sleep."
- **Interpretation:** While coherent, these textbook responses may lack the authenticity you seek.

## GPT Paste Artifacts
- **Question:** "What are your thoughts on continuous learning?"
- **Example Response:** "[Importance of learning], [Skill development], [Adapting to changes]."
- **Interpretation:** Structured, repetitive formats often suggest non-individualized responses, possibly indicative of AI influence.

## Response Pasted
- **Question:** "What innovations excite you the most?"
- **Example Response:** "AI, blockchain, and IoT are pivotal today."
- **Interpretation:** The detection of pasted responses, facilitated by monitoring clipboard actions, is crucial for authenticating original feedback.

## Self-duplicate Response
- **Question 1:** "Describe your typical day."
- **Question 2:** "What does your daily routine entail?"
- **Example Response:** "Yoga, healthy eating, focused work, regular breaks, evening reading."
- **Interpretation:** Repetitive answers to variant prompts may indicate a lack of thoughtful engagement or an attempt to game the system.

## Cross-duplicate Response
- **Question (Participant 1):** "What drives you professionally?"
- **Question (Participant 2):** "What inspires your professional growth?"
- **Example Response:** "Challenge, learning, innovation, making an impact."
- **Interpretation:** Mirroring responses across participants can signal collusion or shared answers, detracting from the survey's uniqueness.

Employ this guide as a strategic companion to navigate the complexities of survey response analysis, ensuring that your investments in fraud and bot detection yield accurate, actionable, and authentic insights.
